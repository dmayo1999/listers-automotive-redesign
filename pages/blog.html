What Does Your Oil Color Tell You?

Motor oil changes color as it ages and collects debris. Amber means fresh, dark brown means it's getting old, and black means it's well past due. Here's a full breakdown by color and what action to take.

Amber / Light Brown: Fresh oil — you're good. Dark Brown: Oil is aging, plan an oil change soon. Black: Overdue — change it now. Milky / Frothy: Potential coolant leak into the oil — bring it in immediately.

Signs Your Suspension Needs Attention

Your suspension keeps your tires in contact with the road. When it starts going, you'll feel it — and ignoring it costs more in the long run.

  • → Vehicle pulls to one side when driving straight
  • → Rough, bouncy ride over normal roads
  • → Nose dives forward when braking
  • → Uneven tire wear
  • → Clunking or squeaking over bumps

How Often Should You Change Your Oil?

The old "every 3,000 miles" rule is outdated. Modern vehicles and synthetic oil have changed the math significantly.

  • → Conventional oil: 3,000–5,000 miles
  • → Synthetic blend: 5,000–7,500 miles
  • → Full synthetic: 7,500–10,000+ miles
  • → Always check your owner's manual first

5 Signs Your Brakes Need Service

Brakes are your most important safety system. These warning signs mean it's time to come in before a small fix becomes a big one.

  • 1. Squealing or squeaking when braking
  • 2. Grinding metal-on-metal sound
  • 3. Vibration or pulsing in the brake pedal
  • 4. Car pulls to one side when braking
  • 5. Brake pedal feels soft or spongy

Keeping Your AC Running in Florida Heat

In Brevard County, your car's AC works harder than anywhere else in the country. Here's how to keep it running all summer.

  • → Have refrigerant levels checked every 2 years
  • → Replace cabin air filter annually
  • → Run AC for 10 min even in winter — prevents seal drying
  • → Weak airflow + warm air = refrigerant leak sign
